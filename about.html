About Me


Outside of academic and professional life, I have a passion for philosophy. Much of my free time is devoted to reading classic literature and learning philosophy, which in turn I attempt to interpret and elucidate to the reader here.


Most posts here are dedicated to the topic of Nietzsche and tangentially related matters. Hopefully I can share some of my favorite philosophical insights here with you, both from Nietzsche himself, as well as secondary scholarship surrounding the often misunderstood philosopher.


Currently, I am reading through "The Gay Science" (translated by Walter Kaufmann), for the second time. This is one of my favorite works, and many future posts will be dedicated to quotations and material from this book. I plan to cover some of "Daybreak" in the near future as well, depending on my progress here. Later on, I will upload some of my work on Melville that was presented last year at the 2024 National Undergraduate Literature Conference to this page, so stay tuned. Also, I'm really into amateur radio right now, so I might post about that. Feel free to contact me for questions, post requests, or comments.


You can email me at gramma12@msu.edu.


Thank you for reading!
