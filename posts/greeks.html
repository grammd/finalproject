The Values of the Greeks

Published: June 22, 2025 • Author: Dominic Grammatico

"What is required is to stop courageously at the surface, the fold, the skin, to adore appearance, to believe in forms, tones, words, in the whole Olympus of appearance! Those Greeks were superficial – out of profundity!". For Nietzsche, the superficiality of the pre-socratic Greeks was the trademark of their heroic culture, exemplified by Homer and Thucydides. Nietzsche admired the Greeks’ ability to confront the fundamental tragedy and suffering of existence by refusing to lend themselves to a hidden world. Beauty, form, and physicality was epitomized in their art and philosophy. For Nietzsche, this kind of “superficiality” was the response to life’s tragic nature that was the mark of a higher people. It was not until Socrates and Plato did the Greeks look beyond the aesthetic and physical to contend with life.
